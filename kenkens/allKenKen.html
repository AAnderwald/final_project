KenKen: Japanese paper puzzle designed by Tetsuya Miyamoto, roughly translated to "cleverness-cleverness."

Fill each vertical column with every number, no repeating numbers, each puzzle requires a specific order indicated by "cages."

Cages are thick lines that mark off several boxes and have a mathematical equation; they provide the puzzle and the solution.

The numbers & syllables at the top of each cage is the "goal" of the written numbers put inside.

Side note: subtraction & division boxes can be in any order i.e. forget everything you learned in math; a box with "2รท," can be answered either "4 | 2" or "2 | 4."
